Features

Built to feel like a premium remote

A designed control surface — not a thin prototype. Controls are grouped so nothing feels crowded, and only the features your TV supports are ever shown.

⏻

Wake TV

Wake your TV over Wi-Fi (Wake-on-LAN) without reaching for the physical remote. Works when your TV's network-standby setting allows it — on some models this needs a setting like Samsung's "Power On with Mobile".

↻

Auto-reconnect

Pair once and CouchPilot reconnects on its own the next time you open it — no repeated prompts. When the TV turns on again, the app quietly finds it.

▦

The full remote

Power, volume, mute, D-pad, OK, home, back, settings, playback, number pad and color buttons — organised into Core, Navigation and More so you swipe, not squint.

◉

Apps & favorites

Launch apps and pin the ones you use most. On LG, your real installed apps; on Samsung, a curated set of popular apps checked against your TV so you never see a dead tile.

⇄

Inputs & sources

Switch inputs on LG, or open the Source picker on Samsung — whichever your TV supports locally.

☷

Touchpad

A pointer for TVs that support it: move, click and scroll from your phone.

⌨

Keyboard

Type into search boxes and login fields from your phone instead of the on-screen hunt-and-peck.

◐

Volume & mute

Volume and mute with honest state — where the TV reports it, CouchPilot reflects the real level instead of guessing.

▣

My Devices

Save every TV and monitor in your home. Switch between them in a tap, see which is active, and remove any device — all local to your phone.

⚑

Only real features

A capability model shows only what your specific TV supports, so there are never fake or dead buttons.

◇

Multi-brand architecture

One standard remote, trimmed per device. LG is the baseline; Samsung is experimental; more systems slot in behind the same capability model without a redesign.

✎

Compatibility & feedback

Tell us if your TV works, right from the app — privately, with no account and nothing sent unless you choose to.
