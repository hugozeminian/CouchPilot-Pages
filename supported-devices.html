Compatibility

Supported devices

CouchPilot is a local remote — your phone talks to your TV over your home Wi-Fi. Whether it works depends on your TV's operating system, not its brand. Many brands ship different systems on different models and in different countries, so we describe support by system.

What the labels mean

Supported

Owner-validated on real hardware. The everyday target.

Experimental

Works on many devices but not fully proven across the range; features vary by model.

Planned

In development or research. Not selectable yet.

Under review

Being evaluated; no decision yet.

Not supported

No safe local way to connect today.

Why brand ≠ support

TCL, Hisense, Philips, Sharp, JVC, RCA, Element and others ship different operating systems on different models and in different countries — the same brand can be Roku on one set and Google TV on another. That's why support is described by system, and why the most reliable answer is often: tell us your exact model.

How to report your device

Open CouchPilot → Compatibility & feedback (from the home screen, My Devices, or Diagnostics). Choose whether it worked, add a note, and send it by email or share. It's private: no account, no tracking, and you see exactly what's sent — never your IP address, MAC address, or any key. Your reports are how this list grows.

A system moves from Experimental to Supported only after owner validation on more than one device. This page is checked against our validation records before every update.

Good to know

  • Your phone and TV must be on the same Wi-Fi network.
  • Features vary by model, even within a supported system.
  • CouchPilot is free, with no ads, no account, and no tracking.
